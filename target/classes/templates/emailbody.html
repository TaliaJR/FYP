The email body is one of the two main parts of an e-mail, with the other being the header. The body of an e-mail contains the actual message received from the sender plus any attachments that have been sent along with the message such as pictures and files. The e-mail body can contain links to websites that look legitimate but have actually been set up by an attacker to try and steal a user's credentials. If these false websites have been set up by the phisher, it may be possible to get more information about them by investigating the website. The attachments sent by the phisher may contain malware that may steal your information or even compromise your computer.
