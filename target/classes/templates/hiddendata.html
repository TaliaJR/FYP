Not all data available in an e-mail can be easily found by just reading the e-mail header. This data can be hidden on purpose by the attacker or involuntary by the e-mail server and client. An example of data that could be hidden by the attacker is the sender's e-mail address, this is due to the fact that this can easily forged because e-mail servers do not authenticate the email sender (they do not check whether the e-mail address that the e-mail is being sent from is valid or not). Although it is not always possible to find the attacker's real e-mail address, there are ways to discover whether the address has been forged or not (further information can be found on the "server investigation" page). An example of data that can be involuntarily hidden are timestamps. Timestamps are not always displayed in an easily readable format and there are many different formats used to display time and dates. If a timestamp is displayed in Epoch Second Precision, the date and time 1/1/1980 12:00:00.000 will be displayed as 315532800.
