An e-mail, formally known as electronic mail, is a form of online communication that allows a user to send a message
to another over the internet. Each user is provided with an e-mail address and a mailbox.
An e-mail address is a unique identifier for a mailbox and a mailbox is the destination in which e-mails are delivered,
an example of one can be seen in the image below.

An e-mail address is made up of two parts: the name of the mailbox and the domain of the mailbox.
The name of the mailbox is defined by the user whereas the domain of the mailbox is usually defined by the company
operating the mailbox. These two parts are separated by the '@' symbol as seen in the example below.
