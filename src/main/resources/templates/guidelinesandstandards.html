When analysing forensic material there are certain rules, guidelines and standards that you should adhere to which differ depending on where you live. In the UK, the ACPO Principles of Digital Based Evidence should be adhered to uphold the integrity of the evidence and are as follows:
1. Data from any digital device should not be altered.
2. If it is necessary to alter the data, this must be done by someone who is competent and the reason why the data been altered must be explained.
3. An audit trail for every item of digital evidence should be kept.
4. The person in charge of the investigation has the responsibility to ensure that these standards have been adhered to.
Although these principles are just guidelines (they are not bound by law), it is important to adhere to them so that the evidence's reliability is not questioned in court.
