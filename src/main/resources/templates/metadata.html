The metadata of an e-mail can be found within the email header and body. The e-mail header stores the sender's e-mail address, the sender's e-mail server information and the timestamps of when the e-mail was sent and received. The body of an e-mail address stores the actual message that is sent and any attachment's sent along with it. If the sender's e-mail address is valid (the e-mail address is the same one they originally sent the message from), it can be used to gather hints about the sender such as their name, birth date and location. With the help of the sender's e-mail server, you may also be able to gather personal information about the sender (if they signed up with valid information). The sender's IP address may help you track down where the sender lives and other personal details with help from their internet service provider. If the sender has included a link in the e-mail body to a website that they own or have included their bank account details, these can be used to find the identity of the sender with help from the relevant companies. To see where this information can be found, please visit the pages "The e-mail header" and "The e-mail body".
